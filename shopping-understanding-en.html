Understanding Shopping Search Ads

A comprehensive overview of Shopping Search Ads — ads displayed at the moment of highest purchase intent — covering concepts, automatic keyword matching, campaign structure, and creative strategy.

1 What are Shopping Search Ads?

Core message: Ads shown in the latter half of the purchase decision journey — at the moment when customer purchase intent is highest

An ad product that displays your products to users searching for items on Naver Shopping and Naver Search.

Commerce Customer Purchase Journey

StageBehaviorAd Type
1. AwarenessDiscovers a product through banner/video adsDA (Display Ads)
2. ExplorationSearches Naver for product info and reviewsPowerlink, etc.
3. ComparisonSearches and compares products on Naver ShoppingShopping Search Ads ← here
4. PurchaseSelects final product and completes purchase—

Core Value of Shopping Search Ads

Simultaneously satisfies the needs of both sellers and users:

  • Sellers: Expose their products to users with high purchase intent
  • Users: Discover desired products directly in search results

2 Auto Keyword Matching — Key Difference from Powerlink

CategoryDetails
PowerlinkAdvertiser manually registers keywords
Shopping Search AdsSystem automatically matches keywords based on product information
Structure 
Product information from Shopping Partner Center
  (product name + attributes + category)
          ↓
    Auto keyword matching (system)
          ↓
  Ad automatically shown for relevant search queries

Real-World Example

  • Product name: "Free-range antibiotic-free fertile eggs 20/40/60 count ₩12,000"
  • Auto-matched keywords: eggs, large eggs, fertile eggs, free-range eggs, antibiotic-free eggs, free-range fertile eggs, fertile eggs 40-count, 20-count eggs
💡 The more accurately and richly you fill in the product name and attributes, the more related keywords your ad will automatically match.

3 Campaign Structure

Structure: Campaign → Group → Product/Creative (3 levels)

Grouping Strategy Based on SKU Volume

Choose one of two grouping strategies based on your store type and product lineup:

Case 1. Large SKU (distributors, general shopping malls)

Strategy: Category-based grouping — organize groups by category so that diverse products can each appear for their core keywords

Structure 
Campaign (MO/PC)
  ├── Group: Brand        → Products A, D, G
  ├── Group: Category 1   → Products A, B, C
  ├── Group: Category 2   → Products D, E, F
  └── Group: Category 3   → Products G, H, I

Case 2. Small SKU (single-category stores)

Strategy: Core keyword-based grouping — match popular product Creative A to multiple core keywords

Structure 
Campaign (MO/PC)
  ├── Group: Brand                  → Products A, D, G
  ├── Group: General core keyword 1 → Products A, B, C
  ├── Group: General core keyword 2 → Products A, E, F
  └── Group: General core keyword 3 → Products A, H, I

4 Ad Creative Elements

Basic principle: Shopping Search Ad creatives pull information directly from product data in the EP (product feed). The 4 elements with the greatest CTR impact can be customized at the creative level.

Core Elements

#ElementCTR ImpactDescription
①Product image⭐ HighCompelling image — your USP must be clearly visible
②Product name⭐ HighMust include core keywords in the product name
③PriceModerateSet a competitive price
④Naver Pay pointsModeratePerceived as additional price benefit
⑤Promotional text⭐ HighEnter promotion and additional discount information

Additional Information

#ElementCTR ImpactDescription
⑥Reviews / purchases / likes⭐ HighSynced based on review/purchase volume
⑦Official store badgeModerateConveys credibility as a verified official store
⑧SellerModerateSet accurately to ensure brand keyword matching
⑨Shipping infoModerateBuild customer trust with delivery services
💡 The 4 elements with the greatest CTR impact: ① Product image, ② Product name, ⑤ Promotional text, ⑥ Reviews/purchases/likes

5 Best Creative Guide

2 principles for a high-CTR Shopping Search Ad Best Creative:
  1. An image & product name that stands out compared to competitors
  2. Promotional text & shopping product supplementary info that highlights your store's unique differentiators

Worst vs. Best Comparison

CategoryCharacteristics
Worst Image where the product's USP is unclear / No emphasis on additional elements beyond the product name
Best ① Image with a clear USP (e.g., thick, firm, ergonomic back support)
② Promotional text emphasizing differentiators
③ Shopping supplementary info displayed (star rating, review count, likes count)
